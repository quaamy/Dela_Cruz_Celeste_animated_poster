CELESTE

Topic outline for "Pixels, Persistence, and Power: Feminist Themes in Celeste"

Thesis statement

Celeste, a 2D platformer game characterized by precise controls and obstacle navigation, conveys feminist themes through its narrative and character design, showing how even a genre known for simplicity can explore complex ideas of identity and empowerment.

Topic outline

Introduction

  • Hook: "To use games to tell stories is a fine goal, but it's also an unambitious one" by Ian Bogost
  • Sums up Bogost's claim about the inferiority of games compared to other media for storytelling
  • Sets up the counterargument on how Celeste proves games of any genre can tell ambitious, meaningful stories

Overview of paper

  • Thesis statement
  • First main discussion point: Celeste's narrative within the mechanics
  • Second main discussion point: The story of Celeste
  • Third main discussion point: Madeline's complexity and struggle

Body

Celeste's narrative within the mechanics

  • The simplicity of a 2D platformer game
    • Definition and characteristics of this genre
    • Examples of critically acclaimed yet non-meaningful platformers
      • Super Mario Bros. 3
      • Donkey Kong Country 2
  • Celeste's story being interconnected with its mechanics
    • Climbing mechanics reflect story progression
    • "Dying" and "reviving" as representations of resilience
    • Obstacles symbolizing emotional and psychological struggle

The story of Celeste

  • Beginning of the narrative
    • Madeline's introduction and motivation to climb
    • Establishing the mountain as both physical and symbolic challenge
  • The journey through Mt. Celeste
    • First major trials and the emergence of Badeline
    • Encounters with supporting characters (Theo, Granny, and Mr. Oshiro)
    • Madeline's fall, confrontation, and acceptance of Badeline
    • Integration of abilities after self-acceptance
  • Epilogue
    • Resolution of Madeline's journey
    • Final ascent as a symbol of integrated identity
  • Synthesis
    • The journey of Madeline reflecting her changing mental states
    • The necessity of perseverance and self-acceptance in achieving her goal

Madeline's complexity and struggle

  • Madeline's character design and representation
    • Simple, realistic design emphasizing relatability
    • Her outward appearance reflecting inner uncertainty
  • The identity and role of Badeline
    • Badeline as a manifestation of Madeline's insecurities and fear
    • Conflict showing difficulty of self-acceptance
    • Reconciliation symbolizing growth and self-integration
  • Madeline as a symbol of empowerment and feminism
    • Rejecting stereotypical portrayals of female characters
    • Emphasizing emotional experiences and resilience as feminist traits

Conclusion

Summary of Body discussion

  • Brief recap
  • Importance of Celeste's game mechanics in its storytelling and how it differs from other games
  • The narrative progression of the game
  • In-depth discussion of Madeline's design, psychology, and symbolism

Celeste as a feminist game

  • Narrative emphasizing independence and self-discovery amidst challenges
  • Meaningful representation of female experiences, allowing players to see themselves in the protagonist's journey

Synthesis of paper

  • Answer to "So what?"
    • Establishes the legitimacy of games as mediums that can tackle complex societal issues
    • Shows that emotional and feminist storytelling can thrive in simplistic genres
    • Highlights games' ability to foster empathy and representation for communities
  • Additional implications
    • Encourages more critical examination of games no matter their genre
    • Demonstrates that not only male-centered narratives can succeed
